If you've ever lived in a PG, you know the math.

4 people. 1 tiffin wala. ₹70 each. ₹10 for extra roti.

"Yaar maine paid kiya tha"

Not Splitwise — built for *exactly* this case, not generic expense splitting.

## Version 3 — Contrarian builder bet (best for follower growth)

Every "build a startup" advice says: aim for a billion-dollar market.

I built an app for 4 people in a 2BHK.

Here's why I think it's a smarter bet.

Every month, my flatmates and I have the same friction. Someone paid the tiffin wala. Someone ate extra roti. Someone skipped lunch. Someone shared a sweet.

By the 30th — math nobody wants to do.
By the 31st — someone's quietly annoyed.
By the 1st — "yaar choddo, next month adjust kar lenge".

Multiply that by ~50 lakh PG-living students across India.

Splitwise is too generic. WhatsApp screenshots vanish. Notes apps die in 3 days.

So I built Dabba: one app for one job. PWA, phone + 6-digit PIN (no SMS), one shared ledger, who-pays-whom in one tap. Pair-programmed with AI in ~3 days. ₹0 infra — free tiers only.

My last project, BEBO, was built for the masses.
This one's the opposite — built for 4 specific people who all happen to have the same problem.

Different bet. Boring on paper. Useful in the kitchen tomorrow morning.

## Posting playbook

1. **Upload the PDF, not the images.** LinkedIn → Start a post → 📄 Document →
   `dabba-carousel.pdf` → title it "Dabba: The Tiffin Tracker 🍱". The PDF version
   is swipeable AND the link on slide 9 is clickable. PNG carousels are for
   WhatsApp/Instagram (links don't work in image posts).
2. **The slide-9 link** points to `https://getdabba.vercel.app` — live and
   deployed. If the URL ever changes, edit the href in `carousel.html` (one
   line, marked with a comment) and re-run `node marketing/render.mjs`.
3. **Timing:** Tuesday–Thursday, 9:00–11:00 AM IST gets the best Indian + global
   overlap — but posting energy beats posting schedule.
4. **First hour = everything.** Reply to every comment fast; each reply doubles
   reach. Ask 2–3 friends to comment (real comments, not "Congrats!").
5. **Don't edit the post in the first hour** — edits can suppress distribution.
6. Add your own real numbers when you have them ("our group logged ₹X in week 1") —
   nothing performs like real usage in a follow-up post.
