Why I built a money app for 4 people in a 2BHK

12 June 2026 · Bhavik Thakkar · 4 min read

Every piece of startup advice says the same thing: pick a billion-dollar market. I picked my own kitchen.

Here's the scene. Four of us share a flat. One tiffin wala. ₹70 a box, ₹10 for an extra roti. Someone pays for everyone — it's just easier at the door. Some days you skip. Some days two tiffins get shared by three people because nobody was that hungry. Some days there's a sweet, a papad, a cold drink on top.

Each of those moments is tiny. Multiply by thirty days and four people, and you get the month-end maths nobody wants to do — and the conversation nobody wants to start:

"Yaar maine paid kiya tha." "Haan but tu 5 din tha hi nahi." "Achha chalo, next month settle karenge…"

Next month never comes. Someone quietly loses ₹600. Someone else feels weird. It's never about the money — it's about who has to be the awkward one to ask.

Why not Splitwise? Why not a spreadsheet?

We tried. Splitwise is a great general expense splitter, but daily tiffin isn't a general expense — it's the same transaction every day with tiny variations. Filling a generic expense form for "2 tiffins shared by 3 people, plus one extra roti for Raj" every single day is friction, and friction kills the habit by day four. The spreadsheet survived exactly three days. WhatsApp screenshots scroll away forever.

The problem isn't tracking expenses. It's tracking this one specific expense with near-zero effort, every day, forever.

The one feature that actually matters: nobody can cheat

Money apps for friends live or die on trust. So the core of Dabba isn't the splitting — it's the audit trail:

  • Every entry shows who added it, every change shows who edited it — stamped by the server itself, so it can't be faked even with technical tricks.
  • Deleting doesn't erase. Deleted entries stay visible as restorable "deleted by X" tombstones. Hard-deleting is impossible at the database level.
  • Tap any balance and see the exact entries behind it. Every number is explainable, line by line.

That's what turns "trust me bro" into "check it yourself" — and that's what actually ends arguments.

The ₹0 constraint made it better

I wanted this free forever — for my group and anyone else's. That single constraint shaped the best decisions: phone + PIN login instead of paid SMS OTPs, an installable web app (PWA) instead of ₹2,000+ app-store accounts, and free-tier infrastructure end to end. Constraints breed creativity.

The bet

My last project, BEBO, was built for everyone. Dabba is the opposite: built for 4 specific people — who happen to share their problem with the ~50 lakh students living in PGs across India. Hyper-niche on purpose. Boring on paper. Useful in the kitchen tomorrow morning.

It's open source, and I'm building it in public. Real usage numbers from my own group coming in a follow-up post.

Got a tiffin/mess group? Open Dabba — free, installs from the browser in 10 seconds, and there's a full demo inside that needs no signup. Or ask me for set-up help.
